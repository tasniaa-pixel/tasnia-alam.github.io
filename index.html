Hello!

My name is Tasnia. Welcome to my website.

I am a communication scholar and educator who is currently pursuing a Ph.D. in the School of Communication at the University of Miami. Before pursuing my doctoral studies, I spent more than 15 years in higher education, public policy, international development, and strategic communication across Australia and Bangladesh.

My research sits at the intersection of public relations, political communication, social movements, and digital media. I am particularly interested in exploring how strategic communication shapes public engagement, collective action, and meaning making processes, especially in crisis contexts marked by political uncertainty, media restrictions, and information disruption. My current research examines diasporic strategic communication and public engagement during periods of political unrest and media restriction.

As a mixed-methods researcher, I employ both qualitative and quantitative approaches, including in-depth interviews, thematic analysis, surveys, and content analysis. I am also deeply committed to teaching and mentoring students. At the University of Miami, I serve as instructor of record for undergraduate courses in communication and research methods, helping students develop critical thinking, analytical reasoning, and effective communication skills. Prior to joining the University of Miami, I held academic and professional positions in Australia and Bangladesh. I earned a Master of Public Policy from The Australian National University and both a BBA and an MBA from North South University in Bangladesh.

To learn more about my research, publications, and ongoing projects, or to discuss potential collaborations, please feel free to contact me via email, at txa686@miami.edu
